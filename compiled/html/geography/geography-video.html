Geography Building

✦✦

Welcome to the video gallery of the Museum Building at Trinity College Dublin. Here, you will find a curated collection of videos that explore the building’s architectural significance, rich history, and ongoing role in academic life.

Discover the craftsmanship of Dean and Woodward, the intricate stone carvings of John and James O’Shea, and the breathtaking interior featuring polychromatic stone, marble columns, and a grand central hall. Learn about the building’s continued use as a hub for research and education in geology, geography, and engineering.

Whether you are interested in Victorian Gothic architecture, the artistry of 19th-century stonemasons, or the building’s enduring academic legacy, these videos offer an immersive journey into one of Europe’s most remarkable architectural landmarks.

Architectural Marvels: Delve into the visionary design of Dean and Woodward, the Cork-based architects behind the Museum Building (1853–1857). Learn how their approach to polychromatic stonework and structural elegance made the building a landmark of 19th-century architecture.

Master Stonemasonry: Witness the exquisite carvings of John and James O’Shea, legendary Irish stonemasons whose nature-inspired motifs adorn the capitals and façades. Our close-up footage highlights the incredible detail of their craft, from ivy-clad columns to intricate floral and animal designs.

A Living Academic Space: Beyond its historical significance, the Museum Building remains an active center for education, housing the departments of geology, geography, and engineering. Experience the dynamic atmosphere as students and researchers bring the space to life.

Geological and Cultural Treasures: Explore the geological exhibits housed within the building, including the striking skeletons of male and female Giant Irish Deer in the entrance hall. These relics serve as a testament to Ireland’s prehistoric past and the building’s role in scientific discovery.
