Rubric Building

The Rubrics Building at Trinity College Dublin is a charming, historic red-brick structure that exudes old-world character. As you enter, the narrow wooden staircases creak underfoot, leading to long, dimly lit corridors lined with thick wooden doors. Inside, cozy student rooms feature deep-set sash windows, offering picturesque views of Library Square or New Square. The scent of aged timber and the presence of original fireplaces transport visitors back in time, while the exposed wooden beams in the attic rooms add a touch of Hogwarts-like magic. Outside, the weathered red bricks glow warmly in the Dublin light, standing as a timeless reminder of Trinity’s centuries-old academic tradition.


The Rubrics, constructed between 1699 and 1705, stands as the oldest building on Trinity College Dublin's campus and is Ireland's longest-serving purpose-built residential structure. Attributed to architect Thomas Burgh, who also designed the Old Library, the Rubrics originally formed part of a quadrangle of red-brick residential buildings surrounding Library Square. Over the centuries, it has undergone several transformations, including the addition of a third floor in 1894. Despite these changes, the building has continuously served as residential accommodation for the university community. In 2024, after an 18-month conservation and restoration project, the Rubrics reopened to house new staff, visiting scholars, students, and a new reading room, ensuring its legacy endures for future generations.

A cool fact about the Rubrics building at Trinity College Dublin is that it has been continuously used as student accommodation for over 300 years, making it Ireland’s longest-serving residential building with an academic purpose. Despite its age, it remains a sought-after place to live, with its red-brick charm and central location in the historic heart of the university. It even featured in the TV adaptation of Normal People, giving it a pop culture connection alongside its deep academic history!.

Additionally, The Rubrics is a three-story red-brick building dating back to 1699-1705, with an additional floor added in 1894. Given its approximate dimensions, it likely contains hundreds of thousands of bricks. A standard 17th-century brick size in Ireland would have been roughly 9 inches (length) × 4.5 inches (width) × 2.5 inches (height), with mortar joints between them. Using estimates from similar historical buildings, the total could range between 500,000 to 1,000,000 bricks.
