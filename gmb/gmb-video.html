Graduate Memorial Building (GMB)

The Graduates Memorial Building (GMB) is a striking architectural structure comprising two residential halls flanking a central society hall and debating chamber. This fifteen-bay, four-storey building, complete with attic accommodation, overlooks both Botany Bay and Parliament Square. It's facade, crafted from polished Irish limestone, features central gable-fronts and is dominated by prominent ashlar limestone chimneys with carved string courses, as well as ornately carved limestone urns. Construction of the GMB (Graduates Memorial Building) began in 1892 and it officially opened in 1902. The GMB was built as a visual symbol of Trinity College’s tercentenary—marking 300 years since its foundation in 1592. The GMB stands on the site of an 18th-century Georgian red-brick structure known as the “Rotten Row”, which resembled the existing Rubrics. Funding for the GMB came from Trinity graduates, which inspired its name. It also serves as a memorial to Trinity graduates who died in war, beginning with the Boer War. The architet of the building was Sir Thomas Drew.
